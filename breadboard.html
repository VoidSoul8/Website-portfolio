Breadboards:

What is a breadboard and why are color conventions for wires important?

A breadboard is a prototyping tool used to test electronic circuits in a temporary manner without having to solder anything. Components such as resistors, LEDs, integrated circuits and wires can be inserted into the breaboards holes, allowing circuits to be setup in a quick manner. Wire color conventions are important as they help to verify the purpose of each connection at a glance, to help avoid mistakes from misidentifying wires at a quick glance. Common conventions include red wires for a positive voltage, black wires for groud, and other colors to signify signal lines. Consistent wire colors make circuits easier to understand, debug and maintain as time goes on.
